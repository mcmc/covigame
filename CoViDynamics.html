You're in charge!

Save your population and your economy!

Drag the magenta spot up and down to control the transmission factor.

Try to minimize deaths and minimize economic damage.

You are the leader of your community.
You can decide how much social isolation to enforce, whether to cancel events,
whether people should wear masks, whether they should shake hands, and so on.
All of this turns into a single number: The transmission factor.

The transmission factor is the average number of other
people that an infected citizen will transmit the virus to,
before they develop symptoms themself and either
self-isolate (for mild to "like-a-bad-flu" cases)
or get hospitalized (severe cases).

(Of course, many people are asymptomatic and so
will fail to self-isolate, thus raising this average.)

Instead of trying to model social behavior, this simulation
just lets you control the transmission factor directly.

You can decide whether to focus more on deaths or more on the economy.
Either way, as you practice, you will see why you need social distancing.

What is the purpose of this game? Do you not take it seriously?

We take it very seriously.
We have been shocked at the degree to which people (including ourselves)
did not understand much at all about the way social distancing measures do or don't help,
what herd immunity is and isn't, what sort of future we can expect under various policy approaches.

The purpose of the game is to help ourselves and other people understand how these factors interact.

For example, if you get good at the game, you will see that your strategy does not depend much
at all on the exact parameters. So even though we don't have good data yet on the parameters
of this virus, we can still implement a close-to-optimal policy by carefully monitoring the
state of our medical system and adjusting the social distancing measures accordingly.

Epidemic vs. Pandemic

The coronavirus pandemic is a pandemic because it is spread over the world.
It consists of many many local epidemics. This games models such an epidemic.
(A future version might model the pandemic nature, with many localized epidemics.)

"Herd immunity" and the dotted yellow line

A dotted yellow line is visible above.
The farther the virus ball is above this region, the faster the epidemic will grow.
The farther the virus ball is below this region, the faster the epidemic will shrink.
If you are very close to the line, the epidemic will stay about the same size.
(Staying the same size means it spreads to new people at the same rate that people recover at.)

When the transmission factor is normal (when the height of the
green curve is where it started), then herd immunity occurs
at the point where the green lines crosses the yellow line.

Herd immunity is not a strategy. It is a phenomenon.
(Just like the pandemic itself is not a strategy. It is a phenomenon.)
Herd immunity (perhaps thanks to a vaccine) is the only way that an
epidemic can end, unless social-distancing measures are kept in place.

(It can also end by eradicating the virus, but that is practically impossible
once an outbreak stops being contained, as is the case here. Even when there
is a vaccine, it is still very difficult. We have been trying to eradicate the
poliovirus for decades, but immunity is still achieved via vaccines.)
